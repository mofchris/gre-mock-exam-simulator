# How to Interpret Your Score Report

Turn a finished mock into a study plan. End result: you know what your numbers mean,
what they don't, and what to drill next.

## Prerequisites

At least one completed mock (see the [tutorial](tutorial-first-mock-exam.md)). Reports
live under **Score History & Review** on the home screen.

## Steps

1. **Read the scaled scores in context.** Each measure is scored 130–170. The report's
   percentile estimates tell you what the number means competitively — Quant percentiles
   run much lower than Verbal at the same score (a 165Q ≈ 76th percentile, a 165V ≈ 96th),
   because Quant scores cluster high. Compare against the averages of programs you're
   targeting, not against 170.

2. **Check the adaptive path before judging a score.** The "Adaptive routing" card shows
   your Section 1 raw scores and which second section they earned. Routing at 10+/12 into
   the harder section unlocks the top of the scale; routing into the easier section caps
   you in the low 150s **no matter how well you do there**. If you're capped, your leverage
   is Section 1 accuracy, not Section 2 heroics.

3. **Treat the numbers as estimates.** This simulator's raw→scaled conversion approximates
   published concordances, and its question mix approximates ETS calibration. Expect
   real-test scores within a few points of a mock here; trust the *trend* across attempts
   more than any single number.

4. **Mine "Accuracy by area."** One topic under 60% is worth a week of targeted drilling;
   uniformly mediocre accuracy means the problem is pacing or carelessness, not knowledge.

5. **Read the pacing card with the 2:30 flags.** A few slow questions is normal. Slow
   questions that you *still got wrong* are the expensive kind — the skill to build is
   recognizing them at the 60-second mark and guessing.

6. **Self-score the essay honestly.** Expand the rubric, read the 0–6 descriptors and the
   sample response, and score your essay where it actually falls. Most first essays are a
   3.5–4.0: clear position, thin examples, no counterargument. Record it in the dropdown
   so it's saved with the attempt.

7. **Work the review, then the deck.** Every miss is already in the **Missed Questions
   Deck** with a full explanation. Reading the explanation now and re-drilling the deck in
   two days (spaced) beats re-reading it immediately ten times.

## Verification

You've done this right if you leave the report with three concrete items, e.g.: "drill
geometry in Tutor Mode," "stop spending 3+ minutes on Quantitative Comparison," "add a
counterargument paragraph to essays."

## Troubleshooting

- **"My raw score seems high for the scaled score"** — check the routing card; you likely
  landed on the easier second section, which caps the conversion.
- **Scores look identical across attempts** — check whether the misses are the same
  *topics*. The score trend chart on the history screen surfaces plateaus; plateaus almost
  always mean drilling hasn't targeted the weak topics yet.
